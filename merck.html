Combination Drug Discovery at Merck

I spent the summer of 2015 at the pharmaceutical company Merck & Co analyzing gene expression data to identify potential immuno-oncology drug combinations that could work synergistically against melanoma.

This isobologram represents the doses of two immuno-oncology therapies required to achieve combination synergy. The diagonal represents the line of additivity (e.g. two drugs have an additive effect), so achieving dose requirements less than that boundary indicates that two drugs are synergistic (my project goal).

Analyzing gene expression data from mouse experiments in response to immunotherapies in three different sensitivity levels revealed the cell types and biological pathways being activated or subdued by the drugs. Pursuing related processes could aid in the development of both diagnostics and therapies for melanoma.
