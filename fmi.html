Precision Medicine at Foundation Medicine

I spent the summer of 2016 at Foundation Medicine analyzing tumor sequencing data for >20,000 lung cancer patients, in an effort to characterize the landsdcape of clinically actionable alterations.

A majority of lung cancer patients have mutations in genes that have been confirmed as tumor initiating genes, many of which can be targeted with current therapies. Of the remaining patients, many have alterations being targeted in upcoming clinical trials, including those patients with a high tumor mutation burden (TMB). Tumor mutation burden has been linked to a better response to immunotherapies, so identifying patients who fit this profile could help match them to effective treatment options where none were previously available.
